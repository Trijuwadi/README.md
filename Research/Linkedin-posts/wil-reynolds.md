## Post 1 — [April 13 , 2026]

[I must say I loved using Claude to help me finally understand crypto a bit better after I just heard about a big 250M fraud. I used Opus 4.6 with extended thinking and it was very balanced, called me out and told me where I might be right.

After about 30 minutes of going back and forth and learning I landed on this statement.

"It doesn't seem like anyone here is doing anything of value, it's all just moving coins around and placing currency bets."

I thought this final response was helpful, please tell me where you think it is way off.

The steelman of your view: after 16 years of Bitcoin and a decade of Ethereum, the dominant use cases for crypto are still 
speculation on crypto itself
leverage on speculation on crypto itself
and laundering the proceeds of the first two.

Most of the "innovation" is financial engineering layered on top of assets whose only real utility is being traded for other versions of themselves.

Drift is a good example — it's a futures exchange where people make leveraged bets on token prices. The tokens they're betting on are mostly other speculative instruments. It's turtles all the way down.

The counterarguments exist and some have merit, so let me lay them out honestly:

**Stablecoins actually do something.** USDC and Tether move something like $20 trillion a year in settlement vol. For someone in Argentina, Nigeria, or Turkey dealing with 40%+ inflation holding digital dollars on a phone is a genuine life improvement. Remittances from the U.S. to Latin America via stablecoins are meaningfully cheaper than Western Union. This is a real.

**Cross-border settlement is genuinely broken in trad-fi.** Sending $10,000 from a U.S. bank to a Philippines bank still takes 3-5 days and costs 6-8% in fees via correspondent banking. A stablecoin transfer does it in 10 seconds for pennies. That's not nothing.

**Bitcoin-as-digital-gold has found an audience.** Whether you think that audience is right or wrong, institutional allocators, some sovereign funds, and millions of individuals now treat Bitcoin as a hedge against currency debasement. You can argue it's a collective delusion, but so is gold if you think about it hard.

Here's the uncomfortable honest take: if you stripped crypto down to only the uses that create real economic value for people who aren't already inside the crypto economy, you'd probably have stablecoins, cross-border payments, and a debatable case for Bitcoin as a store of value. That's maybe 10-15% of the total activity. The other 85% is a global casino with its own parallel infrastructure, its own celebrity culture, its own media ecosystem, and — as you've now seen — its own criminals and its own police.

The defenders will tell you it's "early" and the real utility is coming. They've been saying that since 2013. At some point "early" stops being a defense and starts being an admission.]

## Post 2 — [April 15 , 2026]

[We just asked a room of 200 marketers to enter the same prompt into ChatGPT incognito and I'd say 60% got the same 3 brands. I was shocked to see that level of repetition of brand names.

The prompt was: What are the best 3 banks for sba loans?

This seems to illustrate the power of getting into training data for commercial queries 

The argument for getting into training data is hard financially because the models update so infrequently telling your client we should work on this for 15 months on the hopes we show up in more training data is a tough pitch for 95% of clients.  But if you get in it is like being airbnb to "vacation rentals" your name becomes synonymous with the thing.

If you are a brand who is in training data now for commercial queries you gotta put more into protecting that asset, cause once you lose it, it could be months or years of lost value.

I would bet that tracking a brands loss of or gain
in training data answers for highly competitive prompts should be compared to lead flow on that business line.]

## Post 3 — [April 17,2026]

[Tracking AI visibility is really rubbing me the wrong way lately, but not for the same reasons you've likely heard over and over again ...

Imagine sitting in a room with someone selling billboards and they say "we put up a billboard and more people saw our brand so we're winning" and then you said good and asked for a renewal with no mention of "what did that do for my business"?

None of us would accept that.

I'm seeing so many case studies "we got x more visibility" and every time I'm like, you know models change length of answers, number of brands mentioned and it changes month to month, model to model, and release to release.

Back in November when ChatGPT doubled the length of answers (in our data) - everyone's brand visibility went up.

So the same person in market went from seeing 5 brands to 12, meaning 7 brands just had their AI visibility go up. That's a win for the AI they take to their boss.

But the buyer is still only choosing one of you. 

Right now it's a visibility land grab, everyone (including us) are sending out visibility reports, but I think we owe it to our clients to track leads,sales, anything that holds us accountable to outcomes on a second axis.]
